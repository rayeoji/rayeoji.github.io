About Me

As you hopefully know by now, my name is Raye Oji and I'm currently in my junior year of undergraduate studies at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. I am pursuing a B.A in Computer Science, with minors in Information Systems and French. Unlike most of my peers here at UNC, I am not from North Carolina or even from the United States. I moved to the US at the age of 18 in 2020 to attend UNC, but I'm actually from Nigeria. After I was born in London, UK, my family moved back home to Nigeria when I was three years old. That's where I grew up from ages 3 to 16, in Lagos, Nigeria. At the age of 16, I returned to England for the last two years of high school and completed the International Baccalaureate (IB) program in Oxford. It was after completing high school there that I migrated once again to the US.

Having lived in two epicentres of multiculturalism and economic growth (Lagos & London), I became aware of the growing capabilities of technology at a young age. I knew that I didn't want to be left behind in the rapid technological advancements our society was making, but rather I wanted to be at the forefront. These are some of the reasons I decided to pursue Computer Science and Information Systems. Similarly, I knew that being multilingual would be an invaluable benefit in our increasingly globalized world, which led to my French minor.

Outside of academics, my interests lie in literature and fashion. I am both an avid creator and consumer of literature, and writing is easily one of my favorite pastimes. I also enjoy learning about fashion houses and trends in my free time, and brainstorming ways to incorporate them into my own wardrobe.
